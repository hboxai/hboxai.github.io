PATIENT APPLICATIONS

Obesity 

41% of U.S. adults over the age of 60 suffer from obesity, which can lead to heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes and certain types of cancer. Part of your obese patients’ weight management plan should include what happens between visits — this means regular weigh-ins.Your Patients can Weigh-in @Home and our Weight Scale sends data right to you,so you can monitor weight trends and track progress in obese patients.

Congestive Heart failure (CHF)

Congestive heart failure (CHF) is a chronic progressive condition that affects the pumping power of your heart muscle. While often referred to simply as heart failure, CHF specifically refers to the stage in which fluid builds up within the heart and causes it to pump inefficiently.When a patient has congestive heart failure, 2-3 pounds can indicate an important change in water retention. Gaining a few pounds is the first sign that heart failure is getting worse, so having that information, continuously, about your patients can save lives. Unchecked, this type of rapid weight gain in a patient with congestive heart failure signals that your patient’s body is retaining fluids and you may need to take action.

RApid weight loss

If your patient is losing weight too quickly, you may not know the cause, but you would want to ensure that you take action immediately — whether that’s a phone call or an office visit. Our Weight Scale enables you, to have insight into your patient’s weight between visits through remote patient monitoring so you can monitor weight fluctuations.
